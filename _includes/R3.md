<h2 style="margin-top:40px; color:#333; font-weight:700;">
  How Shared Authorship, Stylometric Style, and Psychological Similarity Relate Across Reddit Communities
</h2>

<p>
We are yet to find out how distance types correlate with each other:
</p>

<div style="background:#f4f6fa; padding:16px; border-left:4px solid #4a6cf7; border-radius:6px; margin:14px 0;">
  <strong>R3:</strong> How do the three measures of inter-community similarity : shared authorship, stylometric distance, and psychological distance, relate to one another ? 
</div>

<p>
We want to understand how these distance measures interact. Do they move together in any meaningful way?  
As a reminder, we computed three forms of similarity for every subreddit pair:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Embeddings / shared authorship</strong> (who posts where)</li>
  <li><strong>Stylometric features</strong> (writing style)</li>
  <li><strong>Psychological features</strong> (emotional and cognitive language patterns)</li>
</ul>

<p>
Below, we compare each pair of similarity types to see whether they show any relationship.
</p>

<h3 style="margin-top:30px; color:#d02c2c;">3.1 Shared Authorship Distance ↔ Stylometric Distance</h3>

<div style="background:#fff; border:1px solid #e7eaee; padding:18px; border-radius:8px; margin-bottom:18px;">
  <p><strong>How the distances were built:</strong></p>
  <ul>
    <li><code>embeddings_cosine_dist_df[embeddings_cos_dist]</code>  
      stores cosine distance between subreddit embeddings (range 0–2).</li>
    <li><code>stylometric_cosine_dist_df[stylometric_cos_dist]</code>  
      stores stylometric cosine distance for the same pairs.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div style="text-align:center; margin-top:14px">
  <img src="assets/img/R3/stylo_embeddings.png" width="80%" alt="Combined vs authors+psych logistic boxplots">
  <div style="font-size:0.85rem;"><em>Stylometric vs Embeddings Distances.</em></div>
</div>

<div style="background:#fdf7ef; padding:16px; border-radius:8px; border:1px solid #ffe7c2;">
  The scatter is diffuse, but we see a cluster of points near the lower region that hints at a light trend.  
  A Pearson test confirms it: the correlation is <strong>0.23</strong> (p ≤ 0.05).  
</div>

<p>
In short: subreddit pairs with similar user bases tend to have somewhat closer writing styles, though the relationship is weak.
</p>

<h3 style="margin-top:30px; color:#d02c2c;">3.2 Shared Authorship Distance ↔ Psychological Distance</h3>

<div style="text-align:center; margin-top:14px">
  <img src="assets/img/R3/psycho_embeddings.png" width="80%" alt="Combined vs authors+psych logistic boxplots">
  <div style="font-size:0.85rem;"><em>Psychological vs Embeddings Distances.</em></div>
</div>

<div style="background:#eefbf3; padding:16px; border-radius:8px; border:1px solid #ccefe0;">
  This comparison shows a clearer relationship. The joint plot displays many darker hexagons, and the trend rises from bottom-left to top-right.  
  The Pearson test gives a correlation of <strong>0.49</strong> (p &lt; 0.05), indicating a moderate and statistically solid positive correlation.
</div>

<p>
Communities with similar user bases often show similar emotional and cognitive expression patterns.
</p>

<h3 style="margin-top:30px; color:#d02c2c;">3.3 Stylometric Distance ↔ Psychological Distance</h3>

<div style="text-align:center; margin-top:14px">
  <img src="assets/img/R3/psycho_stylo.png" width="80%" alt="Combined vs authors+psych logistic boxplots">
  <div style="font-size:0.85rem;"><em>Psychological vs Stylometric Distances.</em></div>
</div>

<div style="background:#f4f8ff; padding:16px; border-radius:8px; border:1px solid #d5e4ff;">
  Once again we see a positive relationship, though not as strong as with embeddings.  
  The hexbin plot clusters heavily near the origin, meaning many subreddit pairs are similar in both style and psychology.  
  The Pearson correlation is <strong>0.43</strong> (p ≤ 0.05).
</div>

<p>
Subreddits that write in similar ways often share similar psychological language patterns as well.
</p>


<h3 style="margin-top:30px; color:#d02c2c;">3.4 Summary</h3>

<div style="background:#fafafa; padding:18px; border-radius:8px; border:1px solid #e5e5e5;">
  <p>
    Overall, all three similarity measures are positively related, though to different degrees:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Authorship ↔ Stylometric:</strong> weak alignment</li>
    <li><strong>Authorship ↔ Psychological:</strong> moderate alignment</li>
    <li><strong>Stylometric ↔ Psychological:</strong> moderate alignment</li>
  </ul>

  <p style="margin-top:10px;">
    Similarity in who posts where tends to come with some similarity in how communities write  
    and how they express psychological states, although the effect is stronger for psychological features  
    than for writing style.
  </p>
</div>
